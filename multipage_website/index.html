    Kangaroo Rats

    Kangaroo Rats are nocturnal 'rats' that are native to arid west of the USA. They get their name from
    hopping around like a kangaroo and from being not a rat. Kangaroo Rats have a huge span of elevation
    but usually eat seeds. The rat lives in burrows, with each section having a different purpose.
    they mate in late summer when rain fall is at the highest.

    Their are 22 species of Kangaroo Rats those being;
    The Agile kangaroo rat or Dipodomys agilis,
    The California kangaroo rat or Dipodomys californicus
    the Gulf Coast kangaroo rat or Dipodomys compactus,
    the Desert kangaroo rat or Dipodomys deserti,
    the Texas kangaroo rat or Dipodomys elator,
    the Big-eared kangaroo rat or Dipodomys elephantinus,
    the San Quintin kangaroo rat or Dipodomys gravipes,
    the Heermann kangaroo rat or Dipodomys heermanni,
    the Giant kangaroo rat or Dipodomys ingens,
    the Merriams kangaroo rat or Dipodomys merriami,
    the Chisel-toothed kangaroo rat or the Dipodomys microps,
    the Nelson's kangaroo rat or Dipodomys nelsoni,
    the Fresno kangaroo rat or the Dipodomys nitratoides,
    the Ord's kangaroo rat or Dipodomys ordii,
    the Panamint kangaroo rat or Dipodomys panamintinus,
    the Phillips kangaroo rat or Dipodomys phillipsii,
    the Dulzura kangaroo rat or Dipodomys simulans,
    the Banner-tailed kangaroo rat or Dipodomys spectabilis,
    the Stephens kangaroo rat or Dipodomys stephensi,
    and the Narrow-faced kangaroo rat Dipodomys venustus
