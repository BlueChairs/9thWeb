    Kangaroo Rats

    Classification

    Kangaroo Rats are not Kangaroos nor Rats, they are in a different type of rodent family which also
    includes Kangaroo Mice which are smaller and have a different type of tail. Their closest relative is
    the gopher despite not looking similar.

    Description

    Kangaroo Rats are 3.5-5.5in and weigh 70-170oz. The tail makes up for half its length and the rodent
    has a large head with small ears. The kangaroo rats have small front feet and large back feet as instead of walking
    they jump around on two feet, each foot has 4 or 5 toes depending on the species. The animal ranges
    in color from a cinnamon to dark grey. In all the species of the 'rat' males are bigger the females.
    they will live for 2-5 years.

    Kangaroo Rats usually jump 7ft but can jump up to 9 and can go 6mph. The 'rats' use movement from their jumps
    to be energy efficient. The rat often moves fast and then freezes as a defense against nocturnal animals.

    Environment

    Kangaroo Rats live arid places with sandy/soft ground for burrowing, but can be found from sea level up to 7,100ft upwards.
    The rats are very affected extreme temperature and will stay within their burrows for cold and wet weather. The 'rats'
    burrows have separate room for everything, like sleeping or storage. If they live in colonies that can have from 6 to a couple
    hundred dens. kangaroo rats will block the entrance to their den to keep a relatively same humidity and temperature. They will
    conserve water that will be lost to the desert heat by lowering the metabolic rate as evaporation through skin and from using
    Their digestive system uses the most water. The 'rats' will try to stay within their burrow as much as possible to stay cool.
    They will take dust baths instead as there is not much open water as well.

    Diet

    The kangaroo rats have a diet composed of manly seeds but will also eat insects and greenery. The 'rats' also have a cheek pouch
    that can be used to store food. They will bury seeds for times where there is not enough food and will use different technics when
    types of coyotes, owls, foxes, weasels, badgers, and snakes.

    Social

    Kangaroo rats are solitary and have very little social organization. But the 'rats' will sometimes come together for food.
    Groups of them tend to be very aggressive. Males are more aggressive towards each other when females are more tolerant of other
    females, probably because of how they tend to have overlapping territories.

    Kangaroo rats tend to reproduce in the late summer when rain fall is at the highest but are not limited to only one point of
    the year. The digestion period is 22-27 days with different amounts of offsprings and litters depending on the species.
    young are born inside of a fur line nest inside of the burrow.
